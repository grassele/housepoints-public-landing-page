HousePoints Mobile App: Smarter Chore-Tracking

An app to help parents engage their children in learning accountability and simple financial goal-setting through a system inspired by Hogwarts House points from Harry Potter

Why do current chore-tracking apps fall short?

From a parent's point of view, current apps don't address the need for practical and up-to-date solutions for teaching their kids important life skills such as financial literacy, accountability, and taking responsibility for their actions. They want an effective tool that simplifies teaching these skills in a way that's fun and interactive.

For kids, the approaches used by other apps and traditional chore-tracking or rewards systems are too boring and offer little incentive to stay motivated and engaged in participating. Instead, they want something more exciting and interactive that motivates them to do chores and behave well.

How is HousePoints Different?

HousePoints empowers parents to instill lifelong habits in their children through organized systems for allowances, rewards, and consequences based on their behavior. Drawing inspiration from the Hogwarts house points system in the Harry Potter® series, HousePoints creates a merit-based framework where youngsters can accumulate points for completing tasks and exhibiting positive conduct, while points are deducted for misbehavior and failing to take responsibility. When points are earned, kids have the choice to use them as real-world currency or to continue saving for a greater reward in the future.

General Application Structure

HousePoints was built with the popular RESTful architecture for mobile applications. This means a front-end or client makes requests to the back-end or server, which stores the application data and manages it per the client's instructions.

Back-End

Our RESTful application's back-end code is written in Python. We inherited a basic model of code from some of our project partner's previous work, where asynchronous functions interact directly with the database, performing edits or requesting information from it, and then these functions connect to endpoints, aka URLs that the client can use.

Front-End

For our front-end, we used React-Native, a mobile application client framework that is cross-platform (can be run on both Android and iOS devices). React-Native allows you to implement screens, their visual formats and data population, and navigation between screens. Our application gets populated with data from our backend, so our front-end interacts with the back-end using the URLs for endpoints we created.
