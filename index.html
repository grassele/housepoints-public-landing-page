HousePoints Mobile App: Smarter Chore-Tracking

An app to help parents engage their children in learning accountability and simple financial
goal-setting through a system inspired by Hogwarts House points from Harry Potter

Why do current chore-tracking apps fall short?

From a parent's point of view, current apps don't address the need for practical and
up-to-date solutions for teaching their kids important life skills such as financial literacy,
accountability, and taking responsibility for their actions. They want an effective tool that
simplifies teaching these skills in a way that's fun and interactive.

For kids, the approaches used by other apps and traditional chore-tracking or rewards systems are
too boring offers little incentive to stay motivated and engaged in participating. Instead, they want
something more exciting and interactive that motivates them to do chores and behave well.

GENERAL APPLICATION STRUCTURE

HousePoints was built with the popular RESTful architecture for mobile applications.
This means a front-end or client makes requests to the back-end or server, which stores
the application data and manages it per the client's instructions.

BACK-END

Our RESTful application's back-end code is written in Python. We inherited a basic model
of code from some of our project partner's previous work, where asynchronous functions
interact directly with the database, performing edits or requesting information from it,
and then these functions connect to endpoints, aka URLs that the client can use.

FRONT-END

For our front-end, we used React-Native, a mobile application client framework that is cross-platform
(can be run on both Android and iOS devices). React-Native allows you to implement screens, their visual
formats and data population, and navigation between screens. Our application gets populated with data from
our backend, so our front-end interacts with the back-end using the URLs for endpoints we created.
